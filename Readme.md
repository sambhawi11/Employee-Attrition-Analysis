**HR Analytics Dashboard – Employee Attrition Analysis**
**Project Overview**

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using HR data and visualizes key insights through an interactive dashboard. The goal is to identify factors contributing to employee turnover and help organizations make data-driven HR decisions.

The analysis focuses on variables such as department, job roles, salary levels and promotion delay to understand their impact on attrition.

**Objectives**

- Analyze employee attrition trends.

- Identify departments and roles with high attrition.

- Identify high-risk employees based on promotion delay and job satisfaction  

- Study the relationship between income, experience, and employee turnover.

- Present insights using an interactive dashboard.

**Tools & Technologies**

- Excel – Data cleaning and preprocessing

- MySQL – Data querying and validation

- Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard creation

**Dataset**

The dataset used for this project was provided as part of a mentored data analytics learning program. 
It contains anonymized HR records including employee demographics, job details, compensation, and attrition information.

Key columns in the dataset include:

- Age

- Department

- Job Role

- Monthly Income

- Years at Company

- Attrition

- Gender

**Dashboard Features**

- Interactive KPI cards  
- Dynamic filtering by Department, Gender, Job Role  
- Promotion risk segmentation  
- Attrition trend analysis  
- Executive-level workforce summary  

**Key Insights**

- Certain departments show significantly higher attrition rates.

- Employees with lower income levels tend to leave more frequently.

- Attrition is higher during early years of employment.

- Promotion delay may be associated with increased employee turnover.
